# StudyHall

## North Star

Weekly active students in study servers—a place where remote learners gather to collaborate, share assignments, and stay connected without the noise of general-purpose chat platforms. StudyHall is built for the realities of student life: unreliable internet, need for privacy, and the desire for a community space that actually understands what school feels like.

## Product one-liner

A dark-themed desktop communication platform with group servers, voice/video calls, messaging, and customizable profiles designed for remote student cohorts.

## Target user

Students at all levels in remote education settings—individuals who need reliable, focused group communication for coursework and study collaboration.

## Core bets

- **Academic-specific tools plus offline-first reliability win students from Discord for remote learning.**
  - Falsifier: Students continue to prefer Discord despite StudyHall's academic features and offline capability.

## Named competitors

- Discord
- Slack
- Microsoft Teams
- Telegram

## Differentiation

StudyHall balances Discord's social appeal with academic-specific features (assignment management, class scheduling integration, study group tools) while maintaining superior privacy controls and offline-first reliability—critical for the unreliable internet environments common in remote education.
