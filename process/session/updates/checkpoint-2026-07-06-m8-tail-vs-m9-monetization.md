## Where we are — the good news
The **Educator Tools** track you've been building toward is, in substance, **done and live**:
teacher roles and light moderation, assignment collect-and-return, class scheduling, the
study-group tools (shared timers, study sessions, whiteboard), and direct + group messaging —
all shipped across the last several waves. A class cohort can now run coursework end-to-end in
StudyHall without falling back to Discord, which was the whole goal of this chapter.

What's left on the list is **cleanup, not new features**: a handful of small polish and
test-coverage tickets on messaging, plus one worth-doing reliability fix (making the
"who can I message" lookup hold up on very large servers, which this next wave is handling).
None of it adds a new capability students or teachers would notice as "new."

## The decision that's yours
The next big chapter on the roadmap is **turning on paid tiers (monetization)** — pricing,
free-vs-paid feature split, the business model. That's a product-and-business direction call I
don't make on my own: it has pricing, positioning, and revenue consequences that are genuinely
founder territory. So I'm **not** flipping that switch or closing out the Educator chapter on my
own authority.

Two paths, whenever you're ready to pick:

- **Move to monetization next** — start designing paid tiers and the free/paid line. This is the
  natural "the product does enough, now let's build the business" step.
- **Finish draining the Educator cleanup first** — knock out the remaining small polish/test
  tickets so that chapter closes fully clean, *then* move to monetization.

Other roadmap directions are also queued if you'd rather jump elsewhere: **compliance & data
rights**, **growth / server discovery**, **the offline-first reliability moat**, or
**institution partnerships**.

## What happens meanwhile
The loop keeps running. This next wave ships the large-server messaging reliability fix (real
value, not cosmetic). If you say nothing, I'll keep draining the highest-value remaining Educator
items wave by wave — but I will **not** start monetization or formally close the Educator chapter
until you weigh in. A one-line answer is all I need — e.g. *"start monetization"* or
*"finish the cleanup first."*
