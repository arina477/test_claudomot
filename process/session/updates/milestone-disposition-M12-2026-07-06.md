# A decision for you: the offline-first moat is at a natural finish line

**Date:** 2026-07-06
**What's paused:** nothing is broken — the last piece of work shipped fine. I've paused because the next step is a genuine product call that's yours to make.

## Where we are

The "works offline" advantage is now real and complete for **reading**. A student on a dead or flaky connection can open StudyHall and still see everything they've already loaded:

- their messages and direct-message threads,
- their assignments and class schedule,
- images and attachments they viewed earlier,
- and the whole app opens cleanly cold, with calm, non-alarming wording when something hasn't synced yet.

That's the differentiator you asked for — the thing that helps win students away from Discord — and it's shipped and live.

## The one piece left, and why it's a real decision (not just more work)

When we set the goal for this milestone, it included one more idea: *if the same thing is edited in two places while offline, show a clear screen that merges them back together with nothing lost.*

Here's the catch we hit: **StudyHall doesn't currently have any "edit the same thing in two places" situation.** Everything a student does offline right now is send-only (like queuing a message to go out when the connection returns) — there's no offline editing that could ever collide. So there's nothing yet for a "merge the two versions" screen to actually reconcile. Building that screen first would mean **first building a whole new offline-editing capability** just to create the problem it solves. That's a meaningful new investment, not a small finishing touch — so I don't want to quietly decide it for you.

## Your two options

**Option A — Call the offline moat done, and move on.**
Treat the offline advantage as shipped at its natural finish line (everything readable offline), set the merge-screen idea aside for a future milestone (to revisit if and when offline editing ever becomes a thing), and move on to the next milestone on the roadmap.
*Best if:* you're happy with the offline win as-is and want momentum on the next big theme.

**Option B — Invest in offline editing first.**
Build a new offline-editing capability so that "two versions to merge" becomes a real situation, then build the merge-and-reconcile screen on top of it.
*Best if:* you specifically want students to be able to edit content offline, and see the merge experience as core to the moat.

Nothing else is blocked or at risk in the meantime — this is purely about which direction to point next.
