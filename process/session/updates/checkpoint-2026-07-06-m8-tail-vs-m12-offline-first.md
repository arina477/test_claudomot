## Where we are

The classroom half of StudyHall is essentially built. Across the last several weeks we shipped: teacher/facilitator roles + light moderation, assignment collect-and-return, class scheduling, study-group tools (shared timers, study/focus rooms), direct and group messages, and message search. That was the "academic tools" half of the founding bet, and its headline scope is done — 40 pieces of work completed. What's left under it is three small cleanup items (minor messaging polish); we're draining those now so no time is idle.

## The decision that's yours to make

There are two natural next big directions, and both are founder calls:

**1. Offline-first reliability — "StudyHall keeps working when your internet drops."**
This is the *other* half of the original bet, and the one thing none of your competitors (Discord, Microsoft Teams) offer — a genuinely defensible edge. We were ready to start it on our own, and our internal board largely wanted to. We held off for one reason: **how you'd measure whether it's succeeding is still undefined**, and turning the whole product toward a new multi-month direction is a steering decision we believe is yours. If you say "go," even a rough sense of *what good looks like* (e.g. "students can read and reply to messages fully offline and it syncs cleanly when they reconnect") is enough for us to begin.

**2. Paid plans / pricing.**
A pure business-model decision — what's free, what's paid, and at what price. We're leaving this entirely to you (this is the fourth time we've raised it). We won't decide pricing on our own.

## What we're doing in the meantime

Continuing to ship the small remaining cleanups on the classroom tools, so the product keeps improving while you weigh direction. When you're ready, just point us at offline-first, paid plans, or "keep polishing for now."
